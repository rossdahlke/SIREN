Stanford WhatsApp Study

Research Study Consent Form

Description

You are invited to participate in a research study on the quality of information in messaging applications. You will be asked to answer a series of survey questions. You will be asked to participate in two surveys and download an Android application that passively collects data from the messages (both text and images) you receive in WhatsApp, Telegram, and Signal. Your personally identifying information will not be attached to this collected data. All data analyzed will be de-identified. Upon completion of the study, all collected data will be destroyed (i.e., deleted).

Time Involvement

Your participation will take approximately 30 minutes via the surveys. We also ask that you keep the application downloaded for at least four weeks.

Risks and Benefits

Study data will be stored securely, in compliance with Stanford University standards, minimizing the risk of confidentiality breach. However, there is minimal risk of a data breach. We cannot and do not guarantee or promise that you will receive any benefits from this study.

Payments

You will receive compensation from YouGov for your participation.

Participant's Rights

If you have read this form and have decided to participate in this project, please understand your participation is voluntary and you have the right to withdraw your consent or discontinue participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. The alternative is not to participate. You have the right to refuse to answer particular questions. The results of this research study may be presented at scientific or professional meetings or published in scientific journals. Your individual privacy will be maintained in all published and written data resulting from the study.

Contact Information

If you have any questions, concerns or complaints about this research, its procedures, risks and benefits, contact the Protocol Director, Ross Dahlke (rdahlke@stanford.edu).
